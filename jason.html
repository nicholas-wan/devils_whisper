Background

Jason

Jason is a tech savvy student. He uses smart devices like iphones and Google Alexa. Jason is aware of scams and hacking, and is prudent when receiving emails or messages, especially when they contain links.

July 2020

After a long school day


One day after school, Jason is at home browsing through videos on facebook and instagram. He is in the living room while playing the videos on his mobile phoneâ€™s speaker. Jason's Alexa asks him to update the device software, but Jason was not interested and lazy to do so.

A few months later


Jason continuously did not update his software. Jason was watching some videos on his Laptop again. This time he clicked on a gaming advertisement that led him to another website. He left his laptop alone shortly and left the living room.


What Happened next


The advertisement contained a hidden voice attack that targetted smart home assistants. The aim was to try to obtain the user's personal information or sensitive details. Fortunately, Jason heard his Alexa speaking, and realized that the video was activating the voice commands. He immediately turned it off.

How can we prevent this?


Update your devices to the latest build or software version. Turn off device when not in use.
